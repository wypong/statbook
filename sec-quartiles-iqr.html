    Section 4.1 Quartiles and the Interquartile Range

    Quartiles divide an ordered data set into four parts with about the same number of observations in each part. The first quartile, written \(Q_1\text{,}\) is the median of the lower half of the data. The second quartile is the overall median, so \(Q_2\) is just the median. The third quartile, written \(Q_3\text{,}\) is the median of the upper half of the data.
    🔗
    In this book we will use the same convention as the lecture notes: if the number of observations is odd, remove the overall median before finding \(Q_1\) and \(Q_3\text{.}\) If the number of observations is even, split the ordered data set into two equal halves and find the median of each half.
    🔗

    Example 4.1.1. Quartiles for an Odd-Sized Data Set.

    Consider the ordered data set 4, 6, 7, 9, 10, 12, 15, 18, 20. Since there are 9 values, the median is the fifth value, so \(Q_2=10\text{.}\) After removing the median, the lower half is 4, 6, 7, 9 and the upper half is 12, 15, 18, 20. Therefore
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} Q_1 = \frac{6+7}{2}=6.5 \qquad\text{and}\qquad Q_3 = \frac{15+18}{2}=16.5. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    Example 4.1.2. Quartiles for an Even-Sized Data Set.

    Now consider the ordered data set 3, 5, 6, 7, 9, 12, 14, 18. Since there are 8 values, the median is
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} Q_2=\frac{7+9}{2}=8. \end{equation*}
    The lower half is 3, 5, 6, 7 and the upper half is 9, 12, 14, 18, so
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} Q_1=\frac{5+6}{2}=5.5 \qquad\text{and}\qquad Q_3=\frac{12+14}{2}=13. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    The interquartile range, or IQR, measures the spread of the middle half of the data.
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \text{IQR}=Q_3-Q_1 \end{equation*}
    The IQR is useful because it ignores the most extreme observations and focuses on the central 50% of the distribution. In the previous example with \(Q_1=5.5\) and \(Q_3=13\text{,}\) the IQR is
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} 13-5.5=7.5. \end{equation*}
    A small IQR means the middle half of the data is fairly concentrated. A large IQR means the middle half is more spread out.
    🔗
