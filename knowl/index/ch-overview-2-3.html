Two big ideas appear early in almost every statistics course. Descriptive statistics is about summarizing what the data says right now, often with tables, graphs, averages, or percentages. Inferential statistics is about going a step further and using data to say something about a larger group or about a process that is not fully observed. Much of statistics is about moving carefully from what we have seen to what we think is probably true.
